# Phase 3 — Product Review (durable decisions)

> Review-only phase. This captures the **lasting product decisions**, not the discussion. Lens: "first paying enterprise customer."

## Verdict
**Strong governance core inside an incomplete enterprise/commercial wrapper.** Not enterprise-self-serve-saleable; **conditional-GO only for a hand-held design-partner pilot.**

## Durable findings
1. **The moat is real:** approve → audit → undo → scope, applied uniformly across all three doors. This is the differentiation and it is credible.
2. **Honesty is a strength** ("not tracked yet", "can't generate yet") — it builds technical trust; keep it as a brand principle.
3. **Premium-in-front, utilitarian-in-back:** Home/Built-in AI feel premium; the daily-loop screens (Approvals/History/Access) are still WP-admin-grade. Enterprises judge the screens used 50×/day. *(UX debt.)*
4. **Three structural enterprise gates (at review time):**
   - Built-in AI couldn't be enabled without a developer → **RESOLVED in Phase 4** (in-admin enablement).
   - "Any provider" vs **Anthropic-only generation** → credibility risk; still open.
   - Security review fails on **SSO / least-privilege roles / encryption-at-rest** → still open (post-PMF).
5. **Single-site** contradicts the agency ICP; fleet is a scale-stage need.
6. **No commercial wrapper** (pricing/tiering) — structural for a *paying* customer.

## Decisions carried forward
- Pursue **design partners**, not enterprise procurement, as the next motion.
- Treat governance (not content generation) as the product; content gen is the commodity (#6 pain), not the wedge.
- Defer SSO/RBAC/export/fleet/pricing until after PMF.
