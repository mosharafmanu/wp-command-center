# Phase 5 — Real Design Partner Validation (durable decisions)

> Review-only, run **after Phase 4**. The verdict that should govern the next move.

## Headline verdict
**Stop building. Recruit 3–5 design partners now.** After Phase 4 there is **no product reason** to keep building before partner contact. The remaining risk is no longer engineering — it is **unvalidated demand (N=1)** and the founder pattern of building instead of selling.

## The open question the pilot must resolve
**Wedge ambiguity:** the personas disagree about *what the product is for* — agencies want **Door 1** (safe built-in content/SEO), AI engineers want **Door 2** (governed agents). The product serves both but is **validated for neither.** Run both tracks; let *behavior* decide.

## Persona signal
- **Agency owner:** feels the painkiller (#1 client-site liability); hits the **multi-site** and **Anthropic-only** walls exactly when excited.
- **AI engineer (Claude/Cursor/MCP):** possibly the **fastest, most defensible** wedge (governed agent endpoint); will poke at the platform.
- **Freelancer:** governance is a vitamin — **do not recruit**.
- **CTO:** validates the thesis; flags the demand/PMF risk loudest.

## Readiness scores (design-partner lens, 0–10)
Product 8 · UX 7 · Trust 8 · Demo 6 · Onboarding 7 · Clarity 7 · Differentiation 8 · **Founder Readiness 5** · Design-Partner Readiness 8 · **Commercial Potential 5**. The two telling lows (Founder Readiness, Commercial Potential) are **not fixable by code** — only by talking to partners.

## Top risks (ranked)
1. Demand unvalidated (N=1). 2. Building instead of recruiting. 3. Wedge ambiguity (Door 1 vs Door 2). 4. Anthropic-only blocks first try. 5. Demo depends on founder narration + live API. 6. Name mis-signals "dashboard." 7. Single-site vs agency ICP. 8. "Good-enough" competitors for low-pain users.

## Decision (if it were our money)
**A — stop building and recruit partners immediately.** Not B (one more improvement — the recurring trap), not C/D (redesign/pivot — premature without partner data).

## Go / No-Go
- **GO** — recruit 3–5 partners (weighted to agencies + AI-agent teams; **not** hobby freelancers).
- **NO-GO** — to any further feature building before a partner uses it on a real site.

## The one pre-recruit fix
Not code — **a decision (which door to lead) + a rehearsed, pre-seeded, reset-able demo with the fear-story and a live-API fallback.** That asset moves the needle more than any feature.
