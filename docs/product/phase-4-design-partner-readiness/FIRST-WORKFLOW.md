# First Governed AI Workflow (guided path)

> The demo the whole phase serves: *connect → enable → generate → review → approve → apply → undo.* The Home first-value panel surfaces the **single next step** at each point; this is the full path.

## The six steps

**Step 1 — Connect a provider.**
Built-in AI › Providers → add your Anthropic key → **Test connection** (shows discovered models). Honest note: generation runs on Anthropic today.

**Step 2 — Turn on a tool (in-admin, no code).**
On the same Providers screen, the **Built-in AI tools** card → **Turn on** SEO or Alt Text. The tool now appears as a tab. *(If a site constant/filter controls a tool, it shows "Locked — set in configuration".)*

**Step 3 — Choose test content.**
Pick a post that needs an SEO description, or an image missing alt text. (Home readiness flags if you have neither, with a link to add one — nothing is auto-created.)

**Step 4 — Generate a proposal.**
Open the tool's tab → **Generate suggestions**. The AI proposes a change. **Nothing has changed on your site yet** — it's a reviewable proposal.

**Step 5 — Review and approve.**
Review the suggestion. In Client-safe mode, applying routes through **Activity › Approvals** — approve it, and it applies. The trust strip (*Reviewed · Requires approval · Audited · Reversible*) is visible throughout.

**Step 6 — Undo from History.**
Open **History › Changes** → find the change → **Undo**. The change reverts. That round-trip — generate → approve → undo, fully audited — is the product.

## The 30-second narration
> "AI can change your WordPress site. WP Command Center keeps you in control: it proposes, you review, you approve, it applies — and you can undo any of it from History, with a full audit trail. However the AI reaches your site — built in, an assistant like Claude, or your own app — it goes through the same gate."
