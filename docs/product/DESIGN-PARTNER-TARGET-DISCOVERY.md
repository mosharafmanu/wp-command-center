## 0. The two things to hold in your head before contacting anyone

1. **You are testing a premise, not selling a product.** The premise (PMF Discovery §0): *AI-forward operators are already mutating client WordPress and feel a real fear about it.* If that's false in your reachable network, no outreach volume fixes it — and finding that out in week one is the win.

2. **Your biggest asset (Fiverr Top Rated + a client book) is a trap if used naively.** Fiverr buyers and past clients are warm, real, and reachable — but the overwhelming majority are **buyers of done-for-you work, not operators who run AI agents.** They can validate whether the *workflow output* (safe AI alt-text/SEO) has value. They mostly **cannot** validate the *governance pain* WPCC was built for, because they don't operate the AI themselves. **Use them — but for the right question.** (See D3.)

> **Brutal honesty up front:** the design-partner ICP (AI-forward operators) is *rare* and *not where your warm network mostly lives.* That mismatch is the central execution problem this document solves: mine the warm network for the few who fit, reach arms-length into the communities where the rest actually gather, and use the Fiverr/client channel for a *parallel, faster* test of workflow value.

## DELIVERABLE 3 — Practical Outreach Plan for Mosharaf

> Assets: limited time · solo · no budget · strong WP reputation · agency network · **Fiverr Top Rated profile** · existing client relationships. The plan converts exactly those assets into learning, fastest first.

### 3.1 Contact first (warm + high-pain, this week)
1. **The 5–10 AI-forward operators you personally know** (Targets #1, #2, #4, #5, #9). Warmest + most likely to have the pain. *These confirm or kill the premise in days.*
2. **Repeat content/SEO buyers from your Fiverr history & client book** (#6, #25) — but see the critical reframe in 3.3.
3. **Agency contacts who are genuinely AI-curious** (#18) — qualify hard on "do you actually run AI on client sites?" before investing.

### 3.2 Who to avoid (actively — they waste your scarcest resource: time)
- **Cheap one-off Fiverr buyers** (#46, #31) — no recurring need, won't pay, will happily take free help and teach you nothing.
- **Hobbyists / AI enthusiasts with no client sites** (#43, #44) — flattering chats, zero valid signal. The #1 source of false-positive optimism.
- **Enterprise / procurement-bound** (#41, #42) — months to a "no."
- **Fleet/backup seekers** (traditional agencies wanting MainWP) — wrong job; you will lose that comparison and learn nothing useful.
- **Big-Facebook-group validation** — likes and "cool!" comments are vanity. Don't mistake reach for demand.

### 3.3 The critical reframe on your strongest asset (be honest with yourself)
Your **Fiverr Top Rated profile + client relationships** are your highest-leverage asset — **but for a different question than design-partner recruiting.**

- **Design partners** (operators) validate the **governance pain** — *"I'm scared to let my AI agent touch a client site."* Your Fiverr buyers mostly don't have this pain because *they don't run the agent — you do.*
- **Your Fiverr buyers and clients** can validate the **workflow value** — *"would you pay for safe, reviewable, undoable AI alt-text/SEO on your site, done well?"* — far faster than you'll find rare AI-forward operators.

**So run two parallel tracks, and don't confuse them:**
- **Track A (design partners):** operators from Targets #1–#10 + warm Tier 2. Validates the *pain and the product as built*.
- **Track B (workflow-value, fastest learning):** offer your existing clients/buyers the *outcome* (you run the safe-AI workflow for them, using WPCC behind the scenes). This is a **concierge test of whether the work itself is wanted** — no engineering, just you doing the job. If clients happily pay for the *output*, that's strong evidence the workflow has value even before the product is self-serve. If they shrug, that's a louder negative signal than any operator interview.

> **This is the brutally honest accelerant:** Track B uses your *actual* warm assets to test value in days, while Track A tests the harder governance premise over weeks. Run both. Let Track B's speed de-risk the bet while Track A probes whether the *product* (not just the service) has a market.

### 3.4 Expected outcomes (sober [ESTIMATE — no data])
- Contacting ~15 warm Tier-1/2 people → **~8–10 conversations** → **2–3 design partners** (Track A).
- Offering the workflow as a service to ~10 warm clients/buyers → **~3–5 say yes to a trial** → fast read on whether the *output* is valued (Track B).
- Community outreach (venues 1–4) with the demo video → **1–2 additional arms-length partners** if the video lands.
- **Most likely realistic 30-day result:** 3–5 design partners (mostly warm), 2–4 service trials, and — critically — **a clear yes/no on whether the pain is real.** That clarity, not the partner count, is the deliverable.

## DELIVERABLE 5 — The Exact 10 Categories to Speak With First (in order)

> Ordering logic: **warmest × highest-pain first** (for speed), then **arms-length** (for validity), then **connectors/distribution** (for reach). Front-load the conversations most likely to confirm-or-kill the premise fastest.

| Order | Category | Why this position |
|---|---|---|
| **1** | **AI-forward operators you personally know** (solo consultants/devs already using Claude/Cursor on client WP — #1, #5) | Warmest + the premise is most likely true for them. 3 honest conversations here either confirm the pain exists or reveal it doesn't — the single fastest test you can run. *Start here tomorrow.* |
| **2** | **Technical SEO consultants in your network** (#2) | They own the *killer workflow's* value (SEO meta is their billable service). High pain + high willingness-to-engage. Confirms whether the workflow is worth money, not just interesting. |
| **3** | **Repeat content/SEO buyers from your Fiverr/client book** (#6, #25) — Track B | Your fastest-moving warm asset. Tests whether the *output* is wanted at all, in days. A strong yes here de-risks everything; a shrug is an early, loud warning. |
| **4** | **Accessibility consultants** (#4) | The alt-text on-ramp is undeniable and low-risk; they have a billable use. Easiest "value is obvious" conversation — good for sharpening the demo and earning a quick reference. |
| **5** | **White-label / production-partner freelancers you know** (#9) | Efficiency-obsessed, high-volume, warm. They feel the *grind* the workflow removes; concrete time-savings math. |
| **6** | **AI-curious agency owners in your network** (#18) | Warm and potentially higher revenue — but qualify hard ("do you *actually* run AI on client sites?"). Position 6, not higher, because many will turn out to want fleet (the trap). |
| **7** | **MCP / Claude developer-community members** (#7) | First *arms-length* category. The premise is true for them by definition, so they test whether the pain exists **outside your network** — the validity check your warm contacts can't provide. |
| **8** | **Post Status / serious WP-pro community** (#3, #12, #15) | Arms-length, high-credibility operators. If the demo video lands here, you have market signal beyond your relationships. |
| **9** | **WP educators / dev-rel with audiences** (#14) | Speak to them once the demo is sharp — they're both a signal source *and* a distribution multiplier (one share reaches dozens of Tier-1 people). |
| **10** | **Local-SEO / multilingual agencies** (#8, #10) | Bulk-value buyers, but more friction and less warm. Last of the first-ten because they validate *scale of value* once the core pain is already confirmed. |

### Why this order beats the obvious alternatives
- **Why not lead with communities (cold-ish)?** Because warm conversations return signal in *days*; community outreach takes *weeks* and is noisier. Speed first.
- **Why put Fiverr/clients at #3, not #1?** They're your warmest asset, but they test a *different question* (workflow value, not governance pain). You want categories 1–2 to confirm the *pain* premise before you over-index on the *output* test — otherwise you risk concluding "people want the service" while never learning "people want the product."
- **Why arms-length (#7–#8) before connectors (#9)?** You need to know the pain is real *outside your network* before you spend a dev-rel's audience on it. Validity before reach.
- **Why no enterprise, hobbyists, or fleet-seekers in the first ten?** They're the highest sources of wasted time and false signal. Their absence is deliberate.

## 6. The single honest measure of success for this whole program

Not partners recruited. Not messages sent. Not video views.

> **Can you find, within ~2 weeks, at least 5 people who describe a *real, recent, specific* moment that AI scared them on a client WordPress site — in their own words, unprompted?**

- **If yes:** the pain is real; the design-partner program (and Track B service test) proceed with confidence.
- **If no — if the best you get is "interesting" and "I might use that someday":** the premise is not yet true in your reachable market, and the honest, brutal, money-saving conclusion is **"too early."** That finding, reached in two weeks of conversations, is worth more than any amount of building — and it is the entire point of starting with people, not product.
